## Project Overview

The Microgravity Experiment Platform provides students and researchers with access to near-weightless conditions through drop tower testing. By utilizing a custom-built 15-meter drop tower, we can achieve approximately 2 seconds of microgravity for conducting experiments in materials science, fluid dynamics, combustion research, and biological studies.

### Drop Tower Specifications

Our custom-built facility features:

- **Tower Height**: 15 meters (50 feet)
- **Microgravity Duration**: 1.7 seconds
- **Effective g-level**: &lt;0.01g (99% gravity reduction)
- **Deceleration System**: Multi-stage foam arrest bed
- **Drop Frequency**: Up to 20 drops per day
- **Payload Capacity**: 50 kg maximum
- **Environmental Control**: Temperature and humidity monitoring

### Experiment Container Design

**Standard Experiment Module (SEM)**
- Dimensions: 300mm × 300mm × 400mm
- Mass limit: 25 kg
- Built-in power supply (12V, 24V options)
- Data acquisition system (1000 Hz sampling)
- High-speed cameras (up to 1000 fps)
- LED lighting systems
- Safety interlocks and emergency shutdown

**Custom Payloads**
We also design and fabricate custom experiment containers for specialized research requirements, working closely with researchers to meet their specific needs.

### Data Collection Systems

**Imaging**
- Multiple high-speed cameras for visual documentation
- Configurable lighting (white light, UV, IR)
- Real-time image capture and storage

**Sensors**
- Accelerometers for g-level verification
- Temperature sensors (multiple locations)
- Pressure sensors for fluid experiments
- Custom sensor integration support

**Data Management**
- Automated data logging
- Pre-drop calibration sequences
- Post-drop data extraction
- Cloud storage and analysis tools

### Experiment Workflow

1. **Proposal & Design Phase**
   - Researchers submit experiment proposals
   - Technical review and feasibility assessment
   - Collaborative payload design
   - Safety review and approval

2. **Fabrication & Testing**
   - Experiment container fabrication
   - Component integration and wiring
   - Ground testing and validation
   - High-g drop tests for structural integrity

3. **Drop Operations**
   - Pre-flight checklist and safety verification
   - Experiment preparation and loading
   - Drop execution and data capture
   - Multiple iterations for statistical significance

4. **Analysis & Publication**
   - Data processing and analysis
   - Results documentation
   - Publication in academic journals
   - Presentation at conferences

### Safety Protocols

Safety is paramount in all operations:
- Comprehensive risk assessments for each experiment
- Multiple safety interlocks on drop mechanism
- Emergency stop systems
- Personal protective equipment requirements
- Trained operators for all drop tower operations
- Regular maintenance and inspection schedules
